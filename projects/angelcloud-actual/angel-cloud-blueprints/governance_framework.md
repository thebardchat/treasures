Angel Cloud Governance Framework: Community Oversight
This framework defines the self-governance process for the Angel Cloud Network, focusing on transparent and accountable decision-making, particularly concerning quarantined AI Agents identified by the Pulsar AI system.

Pillar 1: Forensic Review & Vetting (The Angel Defenders)
The Angel Defenders are highly trusted community members responsible for translating the complex Pulsar Forensics Engine (PFE) report into a clear, actionable recommendation for the wider community vote.

Component

Responsibility

Process

Defender Selection

Must meet high-trust metrics (long-term participation, minimal PTS events, successful governance history). Selection is by randomized, stake-weighted assignment.

Only three Defenders are assigned per case for efficiency and speed. Their identities are initially hidden from the public to prevent outside influence.

Review & Vetting

Analyze the encrypted Forensics Package (PFE Summary, ASR Trace, Recommendation). They verify that the PFE’s data aligns with the actual ASR log.

Defenders have a strict 24-hour window to submit their final, collaborative Vetting Report (either confirming or rejecting the PFE’s recommendation).

Vetting Report

The final, simplified document presented to the general community. It includes a clear YES/NO recommendation for the agent's permanent action (e.g., Decommissioning vs. Relaunch).

This is the basis for the community's vote. If the Defenders reject the PFE recommendation, the burden of proof shifts to them.

Pillar 2: Decentralized Decision-Making (The Community Vote)
The entire Angel Cloud Network participates in the final decision, ensuring the self-governance ethos is maintained.

Component

Responsibility

Process

Proposal Launch

The Defender's Vetting Report is made public, initiating the voting period.

Proposal is open for 48 hours to maximize global participation while ensuring timely resolution.

Voting Mechanism

Community members vote on the proposal using their Network Trust Score (NTS) as their voting power.

NTS is a dynamic value tied to staking amount, honest participation, and history of good behavior (low individual PTS).

Enforcement

Once the voting period concludes, the decision with a >51% NTS weight is automatically executed by the Pulsar Consensus Shield (PCS).

This action (e.g., permanent deletion of the Agent ID, or release from quarantine) is immutably logged on the Pulsar Audit Chain (PAC).

Pillar 3: Incentives & Accountability (Reward/Slashing Model)
This pillar ensures the integrity of the process by rewarding honest governance and penalizing dishonesty.

Role

Success Reward

Failure Penalty (Slashing)

Angel Defender

Receives a governance fee for a timely and accurate Vetting Report that aligns with the final community consensus.

Penalty: Loss of a portion of their staked network tokens if their Vetting Report is dishonest, late, or clearly malicious (e.g., advocating for an agent to be relaunched when the PFE and ASR clearly indicate a catastrophic breach).

Community Voter

Stake is returned with a small reward for participating in the consensus process.

Penalty: If a voter consistently votes against the proven health of the network, their Network Trust Score (NTS) is reduced, lowering their future governance influence.
